Prescription Analysis

The computer-based electronic development, transmission, and filling of a medical prescription—also known as electronic prescription, or e-prescribing or e-Rx—replaces paper and faxed prescriptions.

Prescriber

The prescriber is a user of the electronic prescription system who authenticates their identity by signing in through a verification process. Prescribers are typically clinicians or members of the healthcare staff.

Transaction hub

The common link between all parties—prescriber, pharmacy benefit manager, and pharmacy—is provided by the transaction hub. It keeps a list of pharmacies and a master patient index for easy access to the patients' medical records.

Pharmacy

A pharmacy will send a confirmation message as soon as it receives the prescription information from the transaction hub. Additionally, the pharmacy can inform the prescriber that the prescription order has been filled via the system.

Imaging

Following receipt of the prescription, an imaging center will get in touch with the patient to arrange a time for the scan. One benefit of ePrescribing radiography is that patients who are given paper scripts sometimes misplace them or put off calling and making an appointment.
